<h4><strong>YEAHub Activities</strong></h4>
<p></p>
<p>YEAHub activities provide meaningful engagement opportunities for students
beyond regular school hours, taking place every Monday, Wednesday, and
Thursday. These programmes are designed with student interests at heart,
with many activities being student-initiated, to reflect our diverse community's
passions and preferences.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Our weekly schedule includes Sporty Badminton on Mondays and Footballing
Fun on Wednesdays, giving students regular opportunities to stay active
and build friendships through sport. Thursdays offer a diverse range of
activities, featuring both the Chess Interest Club for strategic thinking
enthusiasts and rotating modular workshops such as pottery, tote bag painting,
and hands-on baking experiences. This varied Thursday programme allows
students to explore both intellectual challenges and creative pursuits
whilst developing new skills and discovering hidden talents.</p>

<p>Each semester concludes with educational Learning Journeys that incorporate
Education and Career Guidance elements. Students gain valuable behind-the-scenes
insights through visits to venues such as Bird Paradise and the Oceanarium,
connecting classroom learning with real-world experiences.</p>
<p>A highlight of our programme is the annual 'Be Better' Football Tournament,
where our students participate in friendly matches alongside students from
other schools. This event fosters inter-school connections whilst promoting
sportsmanship and teamwork.</p>
<p>Through these varied activities, we aim to nurture positive growth mindsets
in our students whilst strengthening both peer relationships and teacher-student
bonds, creating a supportive community that extends beyond the classroom.</p>

<h4><strong>Developing Students' Socio-Emotional Competencies and Mental Resilience</strong></h4>
<p>Our Cornerstone Programme includes targeted workshops and groupwork sessions
designed to support students who would benefit from additional guidance
in developing their socio-emotional competencies and mental resilience.</p>
<p>We collaborate with established partners including Boys’ Town YouthReach
and National Institute of Education to deliver these specialised programmes.
By drawing on the expertise of these organisations, we ensure our students
receive high-quality, evidence-based support tailored to their individual
needs.</p>
<p>These initiatives form an integral part of our holistic approach to student
development, recognising that emotional wellbeing and resilience are fundamental
to academic success and personal growth.</p>
